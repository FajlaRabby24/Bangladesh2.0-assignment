"The Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh heads the caretaker government, established to oversee impartial national elections. This non-partisan role, akin to an interim Prime Minister, ensures political neutrality and stability during electoral transitions. Assisted by a council of advisers, the Chief Adviser manages administrative duties without legislative powers. This system, unique to Bangladesh, was pivotal in promoting democratic integrity but was abolished in 2011 following constitutional amendments."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh

Bangladesh is preparing to launch its second satellite, the "Bangladesh Earth Observatory Satellite," within two to three years. The deal for this project is expected to be finalized shortly. This satellite will enhance capabilities in monitoring natural disasters, managing agricultural resources, and assessing environmental changes.

Bangladesh Launches New Satellite

Date: 05/01/2025 Time: 10.25 PM

The first satellite, Bangabandhu-1, was launched in May 2018, making Bangladesh the 57th country to independently operate a satellite in space. The second satellite is expected to further strengthen Bangladesh's position in space technology and contribute to the country's socio-economic development.
